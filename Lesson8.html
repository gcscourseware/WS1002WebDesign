üè¨ 1. Introduction to Wix Stores & WooCommerce

Choosing your e-commerce engine is a foundational business decision. Here is how the two giants compare.

Wix Stores (Built-in)

Type: All-in-one Solution (SaaS)

  • Setup: Extremely fast. Just click "Add Store".
  • Hosting: Included in your monthly fee.
  • Maintenance: Wix handles security and updates.
  • Best For: Small to medium businesses wanting simplicity.

WooCommerce (WordPress)

Type: Open Source Plugin

  • Setup: Requires installation and configuration.
  • Hosting: You must purchase your own hosting.
  • Maintenance: You manage updates and security.
  • Best For: Large stores needing unlimited customization.

Setting Up Payments:

To accept money, you need to connect a processor found in Settings > Accept Payments.

  • Wix Payments / Stripe: Best for credit card processing directly on site.
  • PayPal: Trusted by users, but often redirects them off-site to pay.
  • Manual Payments: Cash on Delivery (COD) or Bank Transfer.

üöö 3. Managing Orders & Customer Interactions

The work isn't done when the sale is made. Fulfillment is key to customer retention.

  1. Order Received: Customer pays, you get a notification. Inventory is deducted automatically.
  2. Fulfillment: You pack the item. In the dashboard, you mark the order as "Fulfilled" and enter the Tracking Number.
  3. Shipping Notification: The platform automatically emails the customer their tracking info.
  4. Delivery: The carrier confirms drop-off.

Save time by setting up automations in your dashboard:

  • Abandoned Cart Recovery: Emails a customer if they leave items in their cart without buying.
  • Thank You Email: Sent immediately after purchase. Great place to offer a discount on their next order.
  • Review Request: Sent 7 days after fulfillment to ask for a product review.
